The HSE approved First Aid at Work three day course covers the same syllabus as the one day Emergency First Aid at Work course but also equips the first-aider with the skills to apply first aid to a range of specific injuries and illness. This course is designed for the larger employer where the staff numbers exceed 50 employees or for workplaces with more than five staff where the risks of injury are higher such as construction, engineering or manufacturing industries.

The First Aid at Work training course will last for 18 hours contact time over the three days excluding breaks and covers the following topic areas:

  • Acting safely and promptly and effectively in an emergency
  • Preventing Cross infection, recording incidents and actions and use of available equipment
  • Cardiopulmonary resuscitation
  • Care of an unconscious casualty (including seizure)
  • Choking
  • Wounds and Bleeding
  • Shock
  • Minor injuries
  • Musculo-skeletal injuries including fractures, sprains and strains
  • Spinal injuries
  • Chest injuries
  • Severe burns and scalds
  • Eye injuries
  • Poisoning anaphylaxis
  • Heart attack
  • Stroke
  • Asthma
  • Epilepsy
  • Diabetes
