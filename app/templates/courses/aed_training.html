This 4 hours course includes basic life support and is designed for people who will have access to an Automated External Defibrillator either through their work or via public access.

With AED's becoming a much more common sight both in the workplace and within public areas, knowing how and when to use one can and does save lives.

No prior first aid knowledge is necessary and the course is run in accordance with best practice and to National Guidelines.

This is also an ideal refresher for existing skills.

The course syllabus includes:

  • Chain of survival
  • Adult CPR
  • Unconsciousness
  • Initial assessments
  • Recovery position
  • Adult one rescuer CPR
  • Heart attack recognition
  • Angina
  • Choking
  • Child and Infant CPR
  • Sudden cardiac Arrest
  • Abnormal heart rhythms
  • Using the AED
  • Pad preparation and placement
  • Safety considerations
  • Hygiene
